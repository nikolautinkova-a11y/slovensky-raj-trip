Getting There

From Prague to Slovenský Raj — ~560 km. Multiple options to mix and match.

🚗

By Car — Fastest Recommended

~6h 15min · ~560 km via D1/D2 highway through Slovakia. Best option with 2–3 cars so there's flexibility at the destination. Park at trailheads (Podlesok, Hrabušice) during hikes.

🚂

By Train — RegioJet

~7h · Praha Hlavní nádraží → Poprad-Tatry · from ~€20–40. 5 trains daily. From Poprad, local bus or taxi to chalet (~30–40 min). regiojet.com

🌙

Night Train

~9.5h overnight · Praha Hlavní nádraží → Poprad-Tatry · from ~€37. Depart late evening, arrive early morning — saves an extra day. Check cd.cz for schedules.

🚂🚗

Auto-Train (Car on Train!) Clever option

Overnight · Praha-Michle → Poprad-Tatry. Load your car onto the train and sleep. You arrive with a car, without the drive. Run by Czech Railways (ČD). More info →

🚌

By Bus — FlixBus / Eurobus

~8.5h · Praha ÚAN Florenc → Poprad · from ~€35–80. 5x weekly. Budget option, but no flexibility at destination. flixbus.com

Day Plans 🥾

A menu of options — pick one each morning based on energy, weather, and how everyone's feeling. No fixed itinerary required.

Plan A — Suchá Belá Gorge
⭐ Most Iconic 🥾 Moderate ⏱ 4–5 hrs total 📏 ~8 km loop

The classic Slovak Paradise experience. A narrow gorge with four waterfalls — the tallest at 29.5m — wooden ladders, bridges, and rock squeezes. One-way up the gorge, return via a separate forest trail. Starts at Podlesok parking.

💡 Start early (8am) to beat the ladder queues. After the gorge, you come out onto a stunning flower meadow — perfect for a picnic.

TrailheadPodlesok parking — Google Maps
HighlightWindow waterfall, tight rock passages, flower meadow at top
DirectionOne-way only up the gorge — entry from below
Best forDay 1 or 2 — accessible, spectacular, great for all fitness levels
Guideslovakation.com  ·  ourwanders.com
Plan B — Prielom Hornadu (Hornad River Gorge)
🚶 Easy–Moderate ⏱ Full day (~6–7 hrs) 📏 Up to 18 km

The longest gorge in the park — 18 km of walking alongside the Hornad River on catwalks suspended above the water, with suspension bridges crossing side to side. You can do the full length or pick a section. Includes the famous Tomášovský výhľad viewpoint: a 200m cliff with Tatra panoramas on clear days.

TrailheadsPodlesok, Letanovský mlyn, or Čingov — can do point-to-point
HighlightCatwalks over the river, Tomášovský výhľad, possible Tatra views
Best forA scenic mellow full day — great for mixed fitness levels
Guideearthtrekkers.com
Plan C — Veľký Sokol Gorge
🥾 Moderate ⏱ 5–6 hrs

A dramatic, wilder gorge than Suchá Belá — and far less crowded. Canyon walls close in tightly above you. Best combined with Tomášovský výhľad for a satisfying full-day loop.

HighlightWild atmosphere, fewer crowds, dramatic canyon walls, open meadows above
Combine withTomášovský výhľad viewpoint for a great full day
Best forDay 3 or 4 — when you want a different vibe from Suchá Belá
GuideKomoot — Top Gorges
Plan D — Via Ferrata Kyseľ
🧗 Challenging ⏱ 5–6 hrs

The most adventurous option in the park — iron rungs, cables, narrow ledges, and exposed traverses through one of the narrowest gorges in Slovakia. Absolutely thrilling for those up for it. Not suitable for everyone — no fear of heights required.

💡 This works well as a split day: adventurous subgroup does Kyseľ while others do Plan B on the same day. Regroup at the chalet in the evening.

DifficultyHard — iron ladder sections, exposed traverses, requires confidence
Best forThe adventurous subgroup — others can do Plan B simultaneously
ImportantCheck current trail status before going — sections can be closed after heavy rain
Plan E — High Tatras Day Trip
⛰️ Moderate–Hard ⏱ Full day 🚗 ~1h from chalet

The chalet's proximity to the High Tatras makes an alpine day trip very doable. Štrbské Pleso lake is stunning in May with snow still on the peaks. Options range from easy lakeside walks to more serious above-treeline hikes.

HighlightAlpine lakes, dramatic peaks, open views — totally different feel from gorge hiking
Best forMid-trip variety, or if anyone wants a classic mountain experience
NoteSome high trails may still be snowy in late May — check conditions

💡 Philosophy: Don't over-plan. Decide each morning based on energy. A slow breakfast on the terrace with no rush is also a valid plan — you're on holiday.

Rainy Day Plans ☁️

Two ready-to-go plans for when the weather turns. Both have UNESCO sites within 40 minutes.

☔ Important: Some gorge trails close or become dangerous in heavy rain as water levels rise rapidly. Always check trail status at the park visitor centre or slovensky-raj.sk before heading out on a rainy morning.
Rainy Day 1 — Dobšinská Ice Cave + Surroundings
🏆 UNESCO World Heritage Site ⏱ ~1.5 hr guided tour 🚗 ~30 min drive

One of the most extraordinary ice caves in Europe — a permanent subterranean glacier with ice columns, frozen waterfalls, and vaulted halls of blue ice. Absolutely otherworldly even on a grey day. It's one of the highlights of the whole trip, rain or shine.

🥶 Temperature inside is 0–3°C year-round — bring a jacket regardless of outside weather!

LocationDobšiná — ~30 min drive from Slovenský Raj
Open in May✅ Yes — May to September season
Book aheadssj.sk — fills up fast, book in advance
After the caveCombine with a lunch in Dobšiná town, or explore nearby Stratenská gorge if rain clears
Combine withSpišský Hrad (if you have energy — it's in the other direction, ~1h away)
Rainy Day 2 — Vrbov Thermal Spa + Spišský Hrad
♨️ Thermal Spa 🏰 UNESCO Castle ⏱ Full relaxed day

The perfect recovery day — thermal pools in the morning to soothe hiking legs, then the jaw-dropping Spišský Hrad (Spiš Castle) in the afternoon: one of the largest castle ruins in Central Europe, perched dramatically on a hill, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The castle is impressive even in the rain.

Vrbov Spa~35 min drive · indoor & outdoor thermal pools, slides, wellness · vrbov.sk
Spišský Hrad~30 min drive from Vrbov · open May ✅ · spisskyHrad.sk
Lunch stopSpišská Nová Ves — good town for lunch, coffee, grocery top-up
TipSpa in the morning (9–12), lunch in town, castle in the afternoon when energy returns

This file is a regular .html file — it opens beautifully in a browser, but to edit it you open it in a plain text editor. Here's how:

1. Find the file slovensky_raj_trip_planner.html in your Downloads or wherever you saved it.

2. Right-click the file → Open with → Notepad (or install Notepad++ for a much nicer experience).

3. Use Ctrl + H to open Find & Replace. Type the text you want to change in the top box, and your new text in the bottom box. Hit Replace All.

4. Save the file (Ctrl + S), then re-open it in your browser to see the changes.

On Mac

1. Right-click the file → Open with → TextEdit. If TextEdit opens in rich text mode, go to Format → Make Plain Text first.

2. Use ⌘ + F to find text, or ⌘ + Option + F for Find & Replace.

3. Make your changes, save (⌘ + S), and re-open in browser.

How to Share It (Google Drive Problem & Fix)

⚠️ Why Google Drive shows code: Google Drive doesn't render HTML files — it treats them as plain text and shows the raw code instead of the webpage. This is a known limitation.

Here are your options from easiest to most powerful:

Option 1 — Netlify Drop ⭐ Easiest

The fastest way to get a shareable link that opens beautifully on everyone's phone:

1. Go to app.netlify.com/drop in your browser.
2. Drag and drop the .html file onto the page.
3. You instantly get a free public URL (like https://fluffy-penguin-abc123.netlify.app).
4. Share that link in the WhatsApp group — anyone can open it on their phone, no app needed.

💡 Every time you edit the file and want to update, just drop the new version onto Netlify again — you'll get a new link to share.

Option 2 — GitHub Pages (Free, permanent link)

If you're comfortable with GitHub: create a free account at github.com, upload the file to a new repository, enable GitHub Pages in settings, and you get a permanent free link. Slightly more setup, but the link never expires.

Option 3 — Keep it local, share the file

Upload the .html file to Google Drive just for storage/sharing, but tell friends to download it first, then open it in their phone browser. On Android/iPhone, you can open a local .html file directly from Files app.
